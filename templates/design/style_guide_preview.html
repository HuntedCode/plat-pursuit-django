Neon / glow — earned, never wallpaper

Glow signals meaning

Luminous cyan-on-dark is our PlayStation-era love letter and our one brand accent for active / in-progress. The rule that keeps it prestigious instead of cyberpunk: glow is earned by state, never painted on surfaces. It marks active, hovered, near-complete, and earn moments — it never washes backgrounds, body text, or resting chrome. Resting = calm matte; glow = the reward energy on top.

Resting · calm matte

Default cards stay quiet and prestigious. Most of the UI lives here.

Active · cyan glow

“Currently in progress” earns the glow. Reserved for meaning.

Hover me · edge glow

Interactive surfaces glow at the edge, not scale.

Do: active/“working on” edges, hover, Horizon near-completion, earn moments — one cyan accent (tier tints only for tier-specific glows). Don’t: glow on every card, neon bars everywhere, glowing body text, full-surface neon gradients (those are the cyberpunk / mobile-game / NFT anti-references our docs call out).

Motion & delight

Reward, never obstruction

Motion celebrates progress and gives feedback. It is additive flair: informational content is fully legible static, and everything here is disabled under prefers-reduced-motion. Principles: weighted, organic, desynchronized — never frantic.
