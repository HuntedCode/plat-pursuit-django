The Frame

The proposed visual primitive for PlatPursuit badges. It's the chrome that surrounds every badge wherever it renders (gallery, share images, comments, profiles, everywhere). This preview shows how it looks across tiers, sizes, and states, plus an exploration of optional cosmetic effects (“flair”) we’re considering for a future customization layer.

Hover, click, and toggle the interactive controls in each section. Feedback prompts at the very bottom.

1. The four tiers

Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum. Each tier carries through the whole card: title bar, art container, and metadata strip all share a tier palette so a Bronze card feels Bronze top to bottom, not Bronze art wrapped in neutral chrome.

11. Key design choices

  • Trading-card layout. Series name at the top (small caps, like a card set subtitle), badge art in the middle, badge name + earn metadata at the bottom. Closer to a Topps baseball card than a Pokemon card. The badge name is the headline; everything else supports it.
  • Corner notches as brand mark. Inspired by graded trading-card sleeves. Small (7-10px depending on tier), tier-colored, and the same diamond shape across every Frame. The goal: a screenshot of any Frame should read as PlatPursuit before any text loads.
  • Tier-tinted everywhere. Title, art, and plinth all share a tier palette (Bronze warm, Silver cool, Gold warm, Platinum cool iridescent). The whole card reads as one cohesive tier color top to bottom, not chrome wrapped around a tier-colored art.
  • Rarity as a feature. The earn-percentage in the plinth is treated as a status indicator, not just a number. Common badges read muted; rare badges glow gold; mythic badges (sub-1%) have an animated pulse and sparkle icon. A mythic badge should stand out in a gallery at a glance, regardless of its tier.
  • Click to flip. Each Frame can show personal stats on the back (earn date, stages complete, rarity rank, next-tier progress). The trading-card metaphor goes from “looks like a card” to “behaves like a card.”
  • Flair = optional, not default. The base Frame is what every user sees by default. The flair effects in Sections 9 and 10 are explorations of a future customization layer where users could unlock and equip cosmetic effects. The base design needs to be strong on its own first.

12. Feedback prompts

The honest gut-checks we’re looking for. Don’t worry about being polite. Conflicting opinions are useful.

  • 1. First impression. Does this read as PlatPursuit? Would you recognize a Frame in the wild as ours, or could it belong to any tracker?
  • 2. Tier identity. Do the four tiers feel distinct enough? Does the tier signaling (notches + chrome tint + ribbon + backdrop) feel right, or is it too loud / too quiet?
  • 3. The badge gallery experience. Section 8 simulates a gallery view. Try the background toggle. Does it hold up at scale? Would you enjoy scrolling through your own badge collection rendered like this?
  • 4. Card flip (Section 5). Is the click-to-flip useful, or unnecessary chrome? What would you most want to see on the back?
  • 5. Flair effects (Sections 9 & 10). Which ones land? Which feel like “too much”? Which would you want for your own badge? Are there missing categories (e.g. nature, weather, magic, sci-fi) you’d expect to see?
  • 6. Deal-breakers. Is there anything in this design you’d push back on hard? Anything that feels off-brand for PlatPursuit?
  • 7. The unknown unknowns. Something you’d expect this design to address that it doesn’t? Edge cases we missed?

This is an interactive HTML preview, not a final implementation. Real badge artwork, real user data, real performance characteristics will all behave somewhat differently when integrated into PlatPursuit proper. Treat the visuals as directional.
