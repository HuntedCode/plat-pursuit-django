Pursuer Card Design Workshop

The Pursuer's trading card. Composes the rest of the kit.

The Pursuer Card is the FOURTH primitive in the kit and the only composite one. It uses Tally for the Master Level, Horizon for the XP-to-next-tier bar, and embeds Frames (at mini variant) for the recent-badge peek row. Sibling to the badge Frame: same family, different content. If Frame is what you've collected, the Pursuer Card is who you are.

Master Level is the sum of all Job levels (RuneScape-style total). At ~20 Jobs × 99-cap each, the theoretical maximum is ~1,980. Tier brackets: Bronze 1–199, Silver 200–599, Gold 600–1,199, Platinum 1,200+. Horizon shows progress within current tier toward the next tier, not progress to the next Master Level — +1 ML is too granular to celebrate.

The card is also the product's strongest cosmetic surface. Section 6 sketches the customization slots: free options provide a base layer of personality; premium adds exclusive options on top, never replaces the achievement tier the player earned.

03 The Five Sizes

Same identity. Five surfaces.

The Pursuer Card has to read as one thing across five different surfaces. Logbook hero. The full card with every layer present. ~320px wide. Where the Pursuer's identity gets its strongest, most-considered moment.

Contains: avatar, name, title, Master Level, tier label, Horizon, top Job, badge peek (3–4).

Pursuer Card primitive (this card's chrome)

The card vessel itself — border, corner notches, tier-tinted backdrop. Same chrome family as the Frame. Defines the composition layout and which sub-elements render at each size.

Tally (Master Level + Job Level)

Bricolage Grotesque, variable-axis tuned for headline scale on the Master Level (96px opsz, weight 800) and section scale on the Job Level (22px). Tabular figures. The numbers feel earned, not reported.

Horizon (tier progression + Job XP)

The Pursuer Card uses Horizon's linear-bar form at row scale (6px tall) for the tier progression and micro scale (3px) inside the Job tile. Color band shifts as the Pursuer closes in on the next tier. Platinum's bar takes a celebratory pulse since there's no next tier.

Frame (recent-badge peek)

The badge thumbnails embed the Frame primitive at mini variant (4 mini-Frames with corner notches in tier color). Production should render the actual components/frame.html partial here, not the workshop's placeholder squares.

06 Customization Slots

Where personality goes.

The Pursuer Card is the product's strongest cosmetic surface — it's on every Logbook hero, every comment, every leaderboard row, every share image. Five slots are exposed for customization. The tier stays earned (driven by Master Level, not bought), but everything else has free + premium expression on top.

This section names the slots; it doesn't workshop every cosmetic option. Each slot is a future-workshop unto itself. Rule: premium options add to the slot inventory; they never override the achievement tier the Pursuer earned.

# Slot What it controls Premium adds
1 Background The card's interior pattern / texture / image. Free: 1–2 default patterns. Default reads as "clean Pursuer Card." Exclusive patterns, animated subtle backgrounds, themed packs tied to seasons / fundraisers.
2 Frame overlay Decorative extra layer around the card's border. Free: none (just the tier border). Premium-only flourishes (engraved patterns, subtle glow rings, edge motifs). Never changes tier color — just adds on top.
3 Particle / animation Subtle ambient motion. Free: none. Default reads as quiet. Subtle particle effects (drifting sparks for Gold, gentle shimmer for Platinum, etc.). Strictly within the kit's motion vocabulary — no confetti, no anti-ref #4.
4 Title plate The visual treatment of the active Title text. Free: plain caps text. Styled title plates (ribbon, banner, etched chrome) earned through milestones or premium packs.
5 Showcase config The badge-peek row layout. Free: 3–4 most-recent badges, default layout. More slots, alternate layouts (pinned favorites instead of recent, themed showcases like "all Platinums," etc.).

07 Anti-patterns

What we are NOT.

The doc names four specific anti-patterns for the Pursuer Card. Each card below pairs the anti-pattern with a brief reason. Shared code-review vocabulary: quote a card by number when flagging a Pursuer Card variant that drifted.

Do not ship

1. Generic "user profile card"

Round avatar + handle + bio + maybe a follower count. The interface of every social app on the internet. The Pursuer Card needs to be instantly recognizable as a PlatPursuit Pursuer — if it could live unchanged on Twitter, GitHub, or Discord, it's anti-ref #1 (too dashboard).

PlatPursuitTrophy hunter · 1.2k followers
Do not ship

2. Card louder than the Pursuer

Heavy frame ornaments, glaring backgrounds, oversized "PURSUER CARD" branding. The chrome's job is to frame the Pursuer, not compete with them. If a viewer notices the card decoration before they notice who the Pursuer is, the decoration is wrong.

Cosmetic overlays should never raise their volume above the avatar + name + level. Premium customization is additive on top of the same base, never replacing it.

Do not ship

3. Inconsistency across sizes

Hero says "PlatPursuit, Gold Pursuer, ML 847." Compact says "PlatPursuit, Level 14 Scout." Mini drops the level entirely. The team can't recognize the same Pursuer across surfaces. Identity backbone (avatar + name + level) must hold at every size; only the optional layers shed.

The five sizes share the same core identity. Drop layers from the outside in (badge peek → top Job → Horizon → title), keep the avatar / name / level intact regardless of context.

Do not ship

4. Customization that fragments recognition

Premium options that replace the tier-chrome rather than overlay it. A Silver Pursuer wearing a "Platinum-style" cosmetic pack reads as Platinum at a glance — even though they haven't earned it. The achievement signal degrades, the social system loses meaning. Cosmetics add personality on top of the earned tier; they never override it.

Tier chrome (border color, corner notches, backdrop) is earned and locked. Customization slots (background, particle, title plate, etc.) are tunable.

08 Decision Criteria

Five tests. Pass all five.

Argue against the criteria, not the candidates.

1Reads as a sibling to the badge Frame

Side-by-side, the Pursuer Card and a badge Frame should look like they came from the same shop. Fails when the chrome diverges enough that a viewer reads "two different design languages." See Section 4.

2Identity backbone holds at every size

Avatar + name + Master Level must read the same Pursuer across hero / compact / profile / share / mini. Fails when the mini variant becomes unrecognizable next to the hero. See Section 3.

3Tier reads on first scan

A Bronze Pursuer and a Platinum Pursuer should be visually distinct at a glance, without reading any text. Master-Level brackets drive the chrome — the chrome carries the prestige. Fails when the brackets are too subtle or too dramatic.

4Customization is additive

Premium cosmetics add to the slots; the achievement tier stays earned. Fails when a premium option lets a Silver Pursuer pass for a Platinum (or any tier they didn't earn). See Section 7, anti-pattern #4.

5Passes the anti-refs

Not a generic profile card (anti-ref #1). Not louder than the Pursuer. Not inconsistent across sizes. Not customization that fragments recognition. See Section 7.

How to use this workshop

  • Sections 1–2 are the canonical primitive + the tier system. Lock the tier brackets and the chrome shift between them.
  • Section 3 is the size adaptation. Five surfaces, one identity. Confirm the content-shedding rules feel right per size.
  • Section 4 is the sibling test. The Pursuer Card next to a badge Frame should pass criterion #1.
  • Section 5 names the composition rules — Pursuer Card composes Tally + Horizon + Frame, doesn't redraw them.
  • Section 6 is the customization surface map. The slots are named; each slot is a future workshop unto itself.
  • Section 7 is shared anti-pattern vocabulary for code review.
  • Section 8 is the rubric. Five tests, pass all five.
