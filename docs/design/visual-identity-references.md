# Visual Identity: Reference Imagery

Curated references for the four signature primitives defined in [visual-identity.md](visual-identity.md). Each entry names a real-world example (a product, game, site, or physical artifact) and a brief note on what it captures.

This is a *working document*, more liable to update than the main visual-identity doc. Add references as we find them, prune ones that turn out to be misleading.

**How to use it:**

- When designing or sketching one of the four primitives, pull up the relevant section here first
- Each reference is a starting point for image collection, a Figma mood board, or a quick Google
- Use the *what to take* / *what to leave* notes to extract the right elements from each reference
- The "Cross-cutting moods" section at the bottom captures references that inform multiple primitives at once (PSN-era influence, the explorer's-office spatial mood)

## The Frame

The PlatPursuit-branded housing around badge artwork. Trading-card chrome, museum case, graded-card sleeve.

### References

**1. PSA-graded trading card sleeves.**
The plastic case + uniform grade label that wraps around varied card art. The grade label is essentially what the Frame does: a small, consistent brand mark + tier indicator + identifying info, around the protected art. Conveys provenance and authentication without ornament.
- *Take:* the uniform brand wrapper around varied content; the small clean tier/grade label; the "this is preserved" feeling.
- *Leave:* literal plastic case skeumorphism; the grading-company branding itself.

**2. Pokemon TCG modern era card frames** (Sword & Shield onward; Scarlet/Violet).
The frame around the artwork conveys rarity and set membership without competing. Holo / full-art / illustration-rare treatments are excellent reference for tier differentiation done modernly.
- *Take:* the way frames mark tier and series cleanly; the modern execution of foil/gleam for higher rarities.
- *Leave:* Pokemon's specific era-of-game flourishes (lightning bolts, energy symbols).

**3. Magic: The Gathering modern frame redesign** (M15, 2014 onward).
Specifically the *borderless* and *extended-art* treatments. The frame as restraint, letting the art breathe but still signaling set membership through small chrome at the bottom.
- *Take:* "frame as restraint" as a design value; bottom-strip metadata treatment.
- *Leave:* MTG's mana cost iconography and color-pie aesthetics.

**4. Apple product packaging** (AirPods Pro box, iPhone box).
The *housing* of a prestige object. Restraint, white space, the product as the hero. The frame here is essentially minimal but conveys the brand aesthetic on first sight.
- *Take:* restraint as prestige; "the product is the hero" mindset; the brand mark doing maximum work with minimum chrome.
- *Leave:* literal white minimalism (we want darker, more luminous).

**5. Criterion Collection film cases.**
Uniform brand frame around radically varied film art. Spine treatment is iconic. The "set membership" mood we want for badge series.
- *Take:* the curatorial-prestige feel; the way a uniform spine creates set identity across diverse content; the typography restraint.
- *Leave:* the specific Criterion treatment (don't ape).

**6. Vinyl record sleeves with strong label identity** (Blue Note Records, Mondo Records, Numero Group).
Frame as label/set identity around varied art. The label brand mark on every sleeve creates instant recognition without overwhelming the cover.
- *Take:* "label as brand mark across varied content"; the elegant restraint of strong label brands.
- *Leave:* music-specific iconography.

**7. NBA Top Shot moments / digital collectible card UIs (the better ones).**
Modern digital trading-card chrome. Cards that breathe in a viewport, with consistent frame language.
- *Take:* digital-native card chrome execution; subtle motion on hover.
- *Leave:* anything that reads as crypto/NFT collectible aesthetic. (Border with anti-ref #2 in the doc; many of these designs cross the line.)

### What to avoid

- Fantasy MMO frames (WoW achievement borders, Diablo legendary item glows, Skyrim parchment)
- Skeumorphic gilded gold-leaf frames
- Generic certificate-of-authentication kitsch
- Heavy ornate scrollwork (period-piece anti-ref)

## The Pursuer Card

The Pursuer's own "trading card." Identity rendering across all surfaces.

### References

**1. Modern Pokemon trainer / avatar cards** (Pokemon Go avatar treatment, Pokemon Sleep avatar, Pokemon Unite trainer cards).
Clean, character-focused, contained. The avatar is featured but the card as a whole is the recognizable artifact. Strong "this is my player identity" framing.
- *Take:* avatar-as-portrait composition; the card-frame language pulling character + level + key stats together.
- *Leave:* Pokemon-specific iconography.

**2. Apex Legends and Overwatch end-of-game player cards / banners.**
The personal banner with name + level + customization that's recognizable in social and cross-game contexts.
- *Take:* the banner format that scales (full / compact / mini); the way customization is *additive* to a recognizable base.
- *Leave:* combat-game aggression in the visual tone.

**3. Steam profile cards / Battle.net account cards.**
The "this is who I am in this system" artifact. Functional, identity-forward, scales across surfaces.
- *Take:* utility-tier identity framing for cross-context recognition.
- *Leave:* Steam's specific functional sterility (we want more warmth).

**4. NBA Top Shot moments** (the player-portrait-in-frame format).
The player + clip presented as a recognizable collectible. Strong "this player is the artifact" framing that maps cleanly to "this Pursuer is the artifact."
- *Take:* player-as-collectible mood; the way framed identity creates social currency.
- *Leave:* NFT visual cues entirely.

**5. Final Fantasy XIV adventurer plate.**
The player's character "card" with class, level, gear, achievements. Identity expression with system structure visible. Good for the "Logbook hero" version of the Pursuer Card.
- *Take:* depth of identity expression; the way structure (jobs/levels) lives alongside expression (frames/title).
- *Leave:* fantasy-RPG visual register.

**6. Topps modern baseball cards.**
Player headshot + stats + team color identity. Unironic, prestigious, sport-card vibe. Critically: the way the *card frame* tells you the year/set instantly.
- *Take:* the elegant pairing of portrait + numerical stats + frame-as-set-marker.
- *Leave:* literal sport iconography; muted tones.

**7. Strava personal bests / segment achievements (the digital "card" view).**
Recent treatments of "you accomplished this" as a sharable card. Modern, restrained, identity-forward.
- *Take:* the modern card chrome around accomplishment data.
- *Leave:* Strava-orange specifically.

### What to avoid

- Generic social media profile cards (Discord, Twitter avatar+handle, LinkedIn) — anonymous, undifferentiating
- Crypto profile/avatar treatments (BAYC, NFT-PFP framing)
- Sterile corporate-identity panels
- Anything that fragments recognition between full/compact/mini variants

## The Horizon

Forward-motion / "what's next" treatment. The signature for path-forward across all progression UIs.

### References

**1. Apple Fitness rings.**
Circular progress that telegraphs both completion and what's left. The "complete the ring" mood is exactly the Horizon's job. The way the unfinished portion glows toward closure is reference-grade.
- *Take:* the close/complete metaphor; warm-as-near-complete coloring; the gentle pulse on near-completion.
- *Leave:* specifically circular geometry (Horizon is more often linear).

**2. Strava activity progress + segment ranking visualizations.**
The ascending climb visualization, summit indicators, "you have X to go." Elevation-aware progress is a great forward-motion analog.
- *Take:* the "summit ahead" mood; the way distance-remaining feels invitational rather than nagging.
- *Leave:* athletic-data sterility.

**3. Duolingo daily goal progress + streak indicators.**
The warm/cool gradient toward goal, "almost there" pulse. Modern execution of progress-forward UI in a friendly tone.
- *Take:* the warm-glow-on-near-completion; the way the next-goal marker sits ahead.
- *Leave:* Duolingo's specific flat-cartoon aesthetic.

**4. Steam achievement progress bars.**
Clean, restrained, readable at a glance. Specifically Steam's "points" progress UI on the recent achievement cards.
- *Take:* the restraint; the way the bar's leading edge subtly hints at "not done yet."
- *Leave:* Steam's overall functional sterility.

**5. Apple Maps "approaching destination" visualization.**
The visual cues that say "you're getting close" without being demanding. Subtle, ambient, never demanding a click.
- *Take:* "ambient nearness" mood as a design principle; the way visual emphasis intensifies near completion.
- *Leave:* literal map-and-route iconography.

**6. Modern open-world game quest trackers** (Witcher 3, Cyberpunk 2077, Horizon Forbidden West).
The "next objective" highlight. Minimal, contextual, doesn't shout. Just enough cue to know where you're heading.
- *Take:* the way the tracker exists in peripheral vision until you choose to focus on it.
- *Leave:* game-HUD aggression.

**7. Steam Deck / PS5 download progress UI.**
Modern progress treatments that feel native to a luminous-on-dark interface. Gleam at the edge, color-coded by state.
- *Take:* luminous-on-dark progress execution.
- *Leave:* OS-specific chrome (and the PSN-derivative concern).

### What to avoid

- Mobile-game "TAP TO CONTINUE" arrows
- Casual game neon "1 STEP CLOSER!" celebrations
- Aggressive notification-dot indicators that demand attention
- Heavy dramatic gradients (the horizon is a hint, not a flag)

## The Tally

Number-as-reward. The signature for how levels, XP, and milestones render.

### References

**1. RuneScape stat panel.**
*The* canonical reference for this primitive. Numbers as identity. The way each level is its own visible badge of effort. A 99 Skill is a meaningful artifact precisely because the *number* is rendered with care. Full stop, this is the closest single reference.
- *Take:* the way numbers carry weight as accomplishments; the visible-progress mindset.
- *Leave:* the specific 1999-era UI execution (we modernize). Don't lift the visual treatment; lift the *philosophy*.

**2. Apple Watch closing-the-rings number animation.**
The count-down/up moment when a goal hits. Weight, satisfaction, finality.
- *Take:* the weight of the number turning over; the satisfaction-beat at completion.
- *Leave:* Apple-specific motion library.

**3. Pokemon Go XP and player level rendering.**
The headline player level rendered with care. Level is the player's identity, displayed prominently, and updates feel meaningful.
- *Take:* "headline-number-as-identity" composition.
- *Leave:* Pokemon-specific colors / stylization.

**4. Final Fantasy XIV battle damage numbers.**
Heavy weight. Numbers feel impactful, not informational. Critical hits get distinct treatments.
- *Take:* the visceral weight; the way different *kinds* of numbers can have different visual gravity (small XP tick vs. level-up moment).
- *Leave:* combat-game register.

**5. Strava personal records.**
"New 10K PR!" with time, presented as accomplishment, not data. Numbers as story.
- *Take:* numbers framed as personal accomplishment, with context.
- *Leave:* fitness-app specific tone.

**6. Pinball machine high-score displays.**
Retro tactile reference. Numbers presented as prestige. Classic high-score-table mood.
- *Take:* the pleasure of a number that *looks* earned; the mechanical-display weight (rendered modernly, not skeumorphically).
- *Leave:* literal pinball UI.

**7. Casino chip stacks (physical).**
Numbers stacking with weight you can feel. Higher stacks read as more prestigious without saying so. Useful reference for "Pursuer Level" as a stackable accumulating concept.
- *Take:* the visual physics of accumulation.
- *Leave:* casino aesthetics (anti-ref overlap with crypto/speculative collecting).

**8. NBA player stat overlays during broadcasts.**
Modern sports-broadcast stat treatments. Bold typography, careful motion. Numbers as identity in a high-stakes register.
- *Take:* numbers rendered with broadcast-grade weight and motion.
- *Leave:* sports-graphic specificity (team colors, league branding).

### What to avoid

- Dashboard metric tiles (Datadog, Mixpanel, Looker) — numbers as data, not reward
- Generic counter-up-from-zero animations (arcade feel, kills weight)
- Spreadsheet cell aesthetics
- Cryptocurrency price tickers (anti-ref)
- Tally treatment applied to *every* count (must be reserved for meaningful numbers)

## Cross-cutting moods

References that inform multiple primitives at once.

### PSN-era informed (the love-letter rule)

References to honor the PlayStation-era visual world without aping Sony's actual UI. See [visual-identity.md](visual-identity.md) §5 for the full love-letter rule.

**1. PS1 startup screen.**
The orange + cyan + abstract-geometric loading sequence. Iconic. Period-defining. The mood is in the *abstraction* and the *limited palette*, not in the specific shapes.

**2. PS2 launch / "I/O screen" (the floating cubes in a void).**
Y2K aesthetic distilled. Crystalline geometry. Soft luminous gradients on deep blacks. Hugely influential reference for the era's visual language.

**3. PS3 XMB UI (cross-media bar).**
Soft glows, restraint, navigation as ambient design. The "luminous accent on dark" palette starts here.

**4. PS4 home dashboard.**
Dark, soft luminous gradients, modernized version of the XMB philosophy. Closer to current PS aesthetic but pre-PS5's specific holographic treatments (which are closer to lawsuit territory).

**5. PSN trophy notification animation.**
What we are stylistically *near* but not directly lifting. Honor the energy without copying the keyframes.

**6. Y2K crystalline / glassy aesthetic broadly** (not Sony-specific).
Frutiger Aero, transparent plastics, blue glows, that 1998-2003 aesthetic milieu. Era-specific and not Sony-trademarked, so safer to draw on.

### What's dangerous (don't go here)

- Triangle/circle/X/square shapes (Sony trademarks)
- The specific PlayStation logo type
- The PS5 home grid layout
- DualSense-specific blue (#0070D1ish) used as a primary accent
- Direct keyframe lifts of the PS5 trophy notification

The test: would this read as homage to a PlayStation player without reading as Sony copy to a Sony lawyer? Both halves matter.

### Explorer's office / collector's space

References that capture the central spatial metaphor: a place where prized artifacts live and the next expedition is being plotted.

**1. Indiana Jones's office (Raiders, Last Crusade scenes).**
Well-curated, framed maps on the walls, artifacts on shelves, the planning desk in the middle. The literal reference for the metaphor (with the caveat that we modernize, not period-piece).

**2. The Criterion Collection's brand environment.**
Curatorial prestige. Restraint. The way a brand can communicate "we take what we collect seriously" without ornament.

**3. High-end audio engineer's studio / mastering room.**
Functional + prestige. Tools of the trade visible but composed. Warm lighting against dark room. Strong mood reference for the Pursuit-as-planning-desk register.

**4. Vintage cartographer's atelier (modernized).**
Maps, instruments, the "in-progress expedition" desk. Strongly supports the *exploratory* adjective and the Horizon primitive's "what's next" mood. Modernize: don't go full period.

**5. Modern museum curator's office.**
Where the curated collection meets the working professional. The public-museum walls + the private workspace, in one. Excellent reference for Pursuit (workspace) + Logbook (curated archive) sitting in the same conceptual room.

**6. High-end watch brand archives** (Hodinkee editorial, Watchwise, manufacturer heritage rooms).
Modern treatment of horological collecting. Reverent without being stuffy. Numbers and provenance treated with care. Strong reference for "modern collector" register without crossing into kitsch.

### Modern collector / archive aesthetics

For the *prestigious*-but-*modern* register that the brand should land in.

**1. The new Pokemon Center websites (post-2022).**
Modern execution of nostalgic collector content. Card-forward layouts, considered chrome.

**2. Mondo Records / movie poster shops.**
Curated artist collaborations, set/series treatments, the "limited and meaningful" mood for collectors.

**3. Neighborhood Goods / curated retail.**
Modern interpretation of curatorial prestige. Restraint + warmth.

**4. The Magic: The Gathering Secret Lair drops site.**
A trading card brand executing modern web design that honors the collectibles. Strong reference for *prestigious + modern + earnest* in a card context.

**5. Hodinkee's editorial design.**
The reference point for "collector content done with serious modern design." Horology, but the principles transfer cleanly.
